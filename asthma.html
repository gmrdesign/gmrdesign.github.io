AIR PLAY

Interactive playground game for asthmatic children

Asthmatic children have difficulty joining in group exercise because of their disease. However, proper exercise are beneficial to asthmatic children. To motimate asthmatic children maintain proper exercise, we designed this immersive game based on interactive playground.

Output: project report

Sep. 2016 - Feb. 2017

Enschede, Netherland


My role:

Introduction of Asthma

Asthma is one of the most common chronic disease. In Netherland, there are more than 70,000 asthmatic children.

Pain points in asthmatic children's activities

We interviewed asthma therophiest, parents of asthmatci children and adults who had asthma in their childhood, and summarized the following features.

  1. Negative Attitude: asthmatic patients have less negative attitudes toward physical activities.
  2. Fear of Onset: the physcial activity could stimulate the onset of asthma.
  3. Shamefulness: asthmatic patients feel embarrased about taking medicine in public.
  4. Subordinate Role: asthmatic patients gradually play the subordinate role in the group physical activities.

Solution: interactive game

We proposed an interactive tagging game 'Air Play' to fully engage asthmatic children and motivate them maintain exercise.

Game concept: Players will be divided into 2 teams, the blue team and the red team. To win the game, a player should chase players from the other team in attempt to ‘catch’ them. A blue player can catch a red player by drawing a closed circle around him. The red player can catch the blue player by tapping on the blue player after collecting enough coins.

Implementation

The game was first tested with paper prototypes.

The game was implemented with Unity and Kinect based on a small interactive playground at the University of Twente.

Usability testings

Children between 7-9 were invited to try out Air Play. A follow-up interview was conducted to better understand their game experience.

In our two rounds evaluation, we used Fitbit to record the amont of physcial activities children did in the game. We observe their sweat condition, their collaboration and their movement.

In general, the AIR PLAY game is fun and engaging for children. Based on our observation, it’s helpful for stimulating physical activity during the game.

Overall Learnings

  1. It's hard for children to tell what they really fear or want. Observe children facial expressions and ask them how did they feel are two ways to find out children' feelings.
  2. Group interview is better for interviewing children because children are less afraid and more likely to talk when they have their friends around.
  3. Gamification design has the broad use in series education and medical treatment.
